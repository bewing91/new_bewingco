SELECTED SCREENS

A seamless 2-player loan application

THE CHALLENGE

Co-located users struggled to fill out an application together

Over 50% of Earnest's cosigned loan applicants lived together and attemped to complete the 2-player application on 1 device. Given the product didn't support this behavior, 30% experienced issues completing their application in this way. We set out to solve this problem to give these co-located users a smoother experience, and ultimately increase conversion.

Existing Experience

EXPLORATION

How might we make it clear whose information is being filled in?

With two users completing an application together on a single device, one UX goal was to make it clear who's information was being filled in at any given time. I explored different ways we could address this including introducing timelines to help locate users, the use of colors to identify which user was active, and a copy strategy that utilized 3rd person to add clarity.

Two concepts ready for research

1. Guided experience

Treats the pair as two distinct users. Tells users who should be entering in information. Relies on colored badges as an indicator of whose turn it is.

2. Open collaboration

Treats the pair as one user. Agnostic to who fills in information. Relies on 3rd person copy as an indicator of whose information is being entered.

RESEARCH

Understanding how students and cosigners work together

With two prototypes in hand, we interviewed 10 pairs of co-located students and cosigners in order to understand how they filled out applications together. Based on these conversations, I came away with one key insight that informed my designs:

Every student-cosigner relationship is different

Who drives the process
Sometimes the student is leading the charge; other times the cosigner.
How information is shared
Some pairs feel comfortable sharing sensitive information with each other; others didn't.
How the device is shared
Some pairs pass a laptop back and forth, others have one person doing all of the typing.
CONVERGING ON A SOLUTION

A flexible, transparent application flow

Given that every student-cosigner relationship is different, it was clear we needed to make the experience flexible enough to accomodate a wide range of user behaviors while making it clear who's information was needed at any given point. To meet this need, I utilized elements from both concepts including: colorful avatars to represent users, animations to highlight transitions, timelines to locate users, and third-person copy to make who was entering info clear and flexibile.


POST-LAUNCH

Learning and Iterating

Upon launch, we saw some positive signs: about 85% of users were choosing this co-located flow, more than we anticipated. Unfortunately we didn't get the conversion lift we expected. After an investigation, we learned that the quality of cosigners being added was lower in this co-located flow, potentially due to reduced friction, and was impacting our conversion.

We hypothesized a stronger filtering mechanism at the top of the funnel would help solve these issues, and added a soft-pull rate check to our roadmap in part to address this.
