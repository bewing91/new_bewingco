SELECTED SCREENS

A simple way to select the right loan and apply for it

THE CHALLENGE

Parents needed to be able to select and apply for the right loan

To meet an unmet need in education financing and to capture additional volume via a partnership agreement, Earnest wanted to launch a new Parent Loan product. With the addition of this new loan, users would have a total of 3 types to pick from. In order to ensure that users were able to select the right type of loan for them and apply for the Parent Loan when that was the best option, I needed to both redesign our top of funnel for all of our applications and design a smooth application for Parent Loans.

STRATEGY

An MVP to Build Upon

Due to marketing partnership dependencies, the timeline for the Parent Loan project was relatively short. With that in mind, we focused on delivering an MVP that was able to direct applicants to the best loan for them and, should they choose a parent loan, make it a quick and simple application process. To cut down on engineering cost, I relied heavily on existing patterns and components that were already in production across our existing products and focused on developing an architecture that would meet our needs without adding scope.

RESEARCH

Understanding why parents take out Parent Loans

Given that we were launching a new loan product, we wanted to have a strong understanding of why parents took Parent Loans, particularly over cosigning a Student Loan. We conducted a round of exploratory research, interviewing 10 parents: 5 who took Parent Loans, and 5 who cosigned a Student Loan after considering a Parent Loan. Based on these conversations we learned a couple key things:

Few parents know off the bat that they want parent loans
Savvy parents looked for lowest rate regardless of loan type. Others took parent loans as a last resort.
Parents choose cosigned loans to build their child's credit
Parents who considered both Parent and Cosigned Loans and went with Cosigned did so to help their child build credit.
EXPLORATION: TOP OF FUNNEL

How might we make it easy for users to select the right loan for them?

In adding a new loan product to Earnest's offering, I needed to reassess and restructure the top of funnel of our applications to ensure that users were able to select and apply for the right loan. I outlined the personas that needed to be accounted for and explored some architectures for the top of funnel. We ultimately landed on the Two-Step as the best option to serve all personas (especially given the breakdown of the personas and additional engineering constraints).

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

Structuring the application to be quick and clear

In order to make the application quick and clear for the parents filling it out, I explored four different flow architectures with various form segmentations. Ultimately we decided to move forward with the Single Student Form architecture as it offered clarity with separate forms for each user, without fragmenting the experience too much.

OUTCOME

A successful launch and first season

The parent loan product launched in June of 2021 and was a big success. It met our loan volume targets for the year and converted ~40% higher than our other application flows. The changes to the top of funnel likewise met the goal of not negatively impacting our other application flow conversion rates. Most importantly, Parents were able to cover the cost of their child's education in a way that they hadn't before.
