SELECTED SCREENS

A comprehensive and interactive way to manage debt

THE WHY

Customer's #1 request was a mobile app; Earnest didn't have one

Customers were frustated that Earnest did not have a mobile app, so much so that it was the most common request we received. To address this, company leadership set the development of a mobile app as a top priority. I was tasked with leading the design for this initiative, including the definition of strategy and vision, and the design of the app of itself.

THE GOAL

Identify and design the Northstar vision for the app

We knew we needed a mobile app to meet user needs and company goals, but what was it? How was it differentiated from competition? This first phase of the project focused on answering these questions and setting a medium term vision for the app.

EXPLORATORY RESEARCH

Why do users want an app and how would they use it?

To lay a baseline of understanding, we reviewed insights from previous servicing research, conudcted 10 user interviews with users of other loan servicing apps, and surveyed over 600 of our existing users.

Key Takeaways
1. Engaging for control
People engage most frequently (~3x/month) to check status, make payments, and work towards a sense on control.
2. Mobile app for speed and convenience
People cited speed and convenience a key reason they used mobile apps vs. web.
3. Motivated by a sense of progress
People want to see the progress they’re making towards their goals when logging in.
4. Outstanding balance can be a source of anxiety
While it’s a necessary piece of information, their balance can provoke anxiety.
EXPLORATION

Analysis of adjacent fintech apps

Given that Earnest hadn't a mobile app for a few years, we took this as opportunity to survey the fintech app landscape and ensure that we were up to date on the latest conventions to set up our app up for success.

Key Takeaways
1. Content and UI are highly scannable
Use of iconography, color blocks, and focal numbers contribute to clear, scannable hierarchy.
2. Frequently used actions are front and center
Key actions can often be found in floating action buttons or built into nav menus.
3. Touch/FaceID is almost universally offered
This made accessing key information quick and frictionless.
DESIGN SPRINT

Aligning stakeholders on a shared vision

With our insights in hand, we ran a design sprint to align key stakeholders on a vision for the app. This would be a crucial step in making the case to get the resources to build a stand alone team for this initiative. The sprint group included two product managers, our Director of Product Engineering, our Lead Researcher, and Head of Product.


SPRINT OUTCOMES

A goal and a concept

Long term goal

In 2 years time, Earnest will have an end-to-end visualization of a clients journey to help clients understand their finances - before they start with us, during their time with us, and planning for the next.

EXPLORATION

How might we convey a sense of progress?

After the design sprint, I began to explore some concepts for how we might provide a visual, easy-to-undertand dashboard for their journey, and coalesced on 3 concepts to take into research.

Concept 1: Repayment Score
Concept 2: Interest Saved
Concept 3: Paydown

Usability testing Rd.1 takeaways

1. Repayment Score is too abstract
While some liked the score, others felt it was too disconnected from their repayment journey and were confused by not having $’s front and center.
2. Interest saved needed to be in context
People saw interest saved as a positive sense of progress, but it needed to be anchored in the overall payment journey.
3. Paydown preferred for transparency
While seeing their balance can be anxiety provoking, having it front and center is preferred.
REFINING

Honing in on the Paydown concept

Concept 1: Separated
Concept 2: Integrated

Usability testing Rd.2 takeaways

1. Interest saved on it’s own confused some
While some continued to find Interest saved a motivating concept, others struggled to understand the chart on it’s own.
2. Integrated concept preferred
The interactivity of the integrated concept was easiest for people to understand and was more motivating.
3. Payment cards in Integrated concept unclear
Some felt the cards were ads. Others found predetermined amounts too rigid.
FINAL NORTHSTAR DESIGN

A visual, interactive app to manage debt

NEXT STEPS

Paring back for an MVP

With a northstar vision set and stakeholders aligned across the company, we began the process of determining what was feasible for our MVP and developing a roadmap of functionality that would come in later versions.
