A Streamlined way to follow companies

RESEARCH

Understanding B2B salespeople's needs

In order to understand the needs of the users, we conducted a series of 10 user interviews with B2B salespeople. Given that we were initially tasked with designing an iOS app, we were particularly interested in learning about their workflows and behaviors, and where (mobile vs desktop) they completed different activities.

Based on these conversations, we came away with some key insights:

  • Salespeople prefer to conduct research on their desktops
  • Salespeople monitor a list of companies and respond to updates about these companies
  • Notification needs and preferences vary. Salespeople rarely need to respond immediately.

After considering these insights and the Craft's goals and needs, we decided that improvements to Craft's new feed functionality would best create value for users and the business.

Identifying usability problems

To identify problems in the Craft feed, we conducted usability tests with 5 people. After uncovering a slew of issues in the current feed experience, we prioritized them using a 2x2 and plotted each problem based on ease of implementation and impact on the user.

We came away with 3 key problems to address:

Users struggle to stay up-to-date on companies they care about.
  1. Finding companies to follow is confusing.
  2. Following companies is confusing.
  3. Identifying information in the feed is difficult.
Pain Point 1: Finding companies to follow is confusing.

Solution: How might we make it easy to find companies to follow?

Before: Two Searches, Two Purposes
  • Page had two search bars with different behaviors and functionalities.
  • Users who used top search were taken away from feed and struggled to find following button.
  • Only 1/5 users tested thought to use the correct search (side search).
Exploring solutions
With Craft's existing design system in mind, we explored solutions to make finding companies to follow easier.

Among the ideas we considered were merging the two searches into one, and adding clearer headers to improve clarity.
After: One Fully Functional Search
To reduce confusion, the searches were combined into one:
  • 'Follow' links were added in top search.
  • Side search bar was removed and the suggested companies list was given a clear header.
  • 5/5 users successfully found and followed companies that they were looking for.
Pain Point 2: Following companies is confusing.

How might we make it easy to follow a company?

Before: A modal with too many settings and CTAs
  • Modal was hard for users to understand.
  • Creating a new list took users to a new screen, leading to increased confusion and dropoff.
  • 0/5 users understood how to add companies to following lists and knew when a company was successfully followed.
Exploring Solutions
We wireframed a few different modal configurations including different options for creating a new list without leaving the modal.
After: A Conversational Checklist
  • Reduced number of CTAs and settings.
  • List creation moved to with-in modal.
  • Header written conversationally for easier processing and to account for both following and unfollowing states.
  • 5/5 users understood how to follow a company, and knew when they had done so.
Pain Point 3: Identifying information in the feed is difficult.

How might we make it easy to understand information in the feed?

Before: Multiple Headings, High Cognitive Load
  • Key information like the company name and type of update split into multiple headers and subheaders
  • Date repeated on news card
  • Confusing information hierachy made it hard for users to quickly understand each update.
Exploring Solutions
After sketching some options, we tested two designs further with 40 people on UsabilityHub.

It became clear that the conversational style header was easiest to understand. It required less interpretation and processing while the button-like nature of the badge design led to some confusion.
After: A Distinct, Conversational Header
  • Headers were consolidated into one conversational style header for easy understanding.
  • Date changed to more familiar format.
  • Users could quickly identify key information for each update.
OUTCOME

To production, and beyond!



Craft's team was so excited about the changes that we made, that they moved them to the top of the roadmap and plan to implement them in early Q4 2019. Thanks to our use of Craft's existing design system, the changes are relatively easy to implement and thus have a potentially high ROI for the Craft platform.

What's Next

In the course of our research, we identifed a number of usability issues with the current feed. While we were constrained by time and engineering considerations, there were a number of additional changes that I would make in order to deliver more value to users and the business. My top priority would be to add filtering functionality to the feed to make it even easier for users to find what they're looking for in the feed.
