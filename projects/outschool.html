Understanding the current application process

Product Audit: A look at the current task flow

The current teacher application process moved prospective teachers between various screens on the Outschool website, emails, and third-party sites. The process felt disjointed and confusing.

Reviewing User Research

Over the past few months, the Outschool Product team collected feedback from teachers and prospective teachers about the application process. After reviewing this feedback, it was clear the prospective teachers were experiencing a few key pain points:

Lack of transparency
Users didn't understand where they were in the process or when they would get a response.
Disparate process
Users didn't understand how or where to continue to the next step of the application process. They needed to move between the Outschool platform, emails, and external platforms
Wasted excitement
Users were excited to engage with the Outschool teaching platform but had no way to while they were waiting.
Job Stories
In order to frame our understanding of users and our design explorations going forward, we developed a few job to be done stories:
  • When prospective teachers are in the application process, they want to know where they are in the process so that they know when they'll be done with their application.
  • When prospective teachers are waiting to be approved, they want to know how long reviewing an application takes so that they know when they'll get a response.
  • When prospective teachers are waiting to be approved, they want to learn how to be a successful Outschool teacher so that they can hit the ground running when they're approved.
