A way to get personalized rates before applying for a loan

THE CHALLENGE

Applicants lacked the confidence to apply

In order to learn what rates Earnest would offer them, applicants had to submit a full application and incur a hard pull on their credit. Given that rate was a key part of their decision making process, many applicants lacked the confidence to submit an application without rate estimates and dropped off. We set out to give users confidence to submit an application by introducing a rate check in the application process.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

Understanding how a rate check fits into our existing application

In order to deliver rate estimates, we needed to restructure the top of our application flow to collect a different set of information and perform a softpull on the applicant's credit. I explored a number of architectures before settling on one that would streamline our application flow while enabling rate check functionality.

EXPLORATION

How might we leverage a rate check to give applicants the confidence they need to finish applying?

Given that users needed rate information to feel confident applying, I began exploring varying amounts of information we could show and ways we could present that information, weighing the tradeoff between providing too much information and bogging a user down vs. too little and not satisfying their need.

3 Rate Result Concepts for Research


Personalized Rate Range

Offers a quick, simple "carrot" to give a sense of personalized rates.

List of Rate Options

Delivers a list of rate options they'll be able to select from.

Loan Customization Upfront

Allows users to customize their rate in 3 steps.

RESEARCH

What information do applicants need to feel confident applying?

By taking these 3 concepts into user interviews, we hoped to learn what information they needed to move forward with Earnest, and what the best way of presenting that information might be. Based on our interviews with cosigners, undergraduate students, and graduate students, we learned:

Specifics are necessary
Ranges don't cut it. Users need specific rates, terms, and payment types, for it to feel personalized and tangible.
Preferences at the outset
Many came in with existing preferences. Rate type preferences were strongest, followed by payment type. Term was fluid.
Selection requires education
Most users will be overwhelmed by selecting loan options without educating content.
CONVERGING ON A SOLUTION

Two rate result concepts to test at launch

From the research, it was clear specific, tangible "offers" were necessary for people to feel that the rate results were personalized and useful, but there were mixed messages on how many options were too many. So after some iteration based on our learnings, we decided to move forward with two concepts and A/B test them as part of the feature launch.

Variation A: Curated sample of options

Tests the hypothesis that showing applicants just a few curated options will give them the confidence they need, while keeping them moving.

Variation B: Full list of options

Tests the hypothesis that applicants need to see the full list of options they'll be able to select from to feel confident.

The Forms

The Softpull Flow

After the applicant fills in their information, we attempt to run a softpull on their credit in order to deliver rate estimates. There are a number of scenarios that can occur that I designed our flow to account for in order to keep the process feeling transparent and seamless to the applicant, and to keep them moving forward.
