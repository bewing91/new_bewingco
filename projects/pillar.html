Three interactive ways to understand student loan debt

RESEARCH

Understanding student loan borrowers' relationships to their loans

In order to understand what information student loan borrowers pay attention to and what they want to know when it comes to their student loans, I interviewed 8 loan borrowers, both in person and via Usertesting.com.

Based on these conversations, I came away with a few key insights to inform my designs:

1. Optimization minded:
Borrowers want to know how much interest is accruing and how to pay off loans efficiently to minimize it.
2. Focus on wins:
Borrowers find their payoff date demoralizing. They want to see what their effort has accomplished.
3. Original balance is a footnote:
Borrowers know their original loan balance and are not interested in it outside of the context of other metrics.
EXPLORING SOLUTIONS

How might we empower borrowers to feel in control of their student loan debt?

Deciding which loan summary cards
Based on our discussions with student loan borrowers, we decided that the three loan summary cards that would best empower them, provide insights, and encourage them to optimize their loans would be:

1. Current Balance:
The Current Balance card will give borrowers a quick and transparent look at their loans and help them feel in control.
2. Amount Saved:
The Amount Saved card will give borrowers a look back at their loan repayment thus far, without demoralizing them.
3. Remaining Interest:
Given that borrowers we talked to were focused on interest optimization, we wanted a card to make that easier.
